Topic Details
Ectopic Pregnancy Definition Implantation of fertilized ovum outside the uterine cavity.
Incidence 0.3-2% of pregnancies, varies with risk factors.
Common Sites Mostly in fallopian tube, especially the ampullary portion; other sites include ovary, cervix, CS scar, rudimentary horn, abdominal cavity.
Etiology Primarily tubal stricture due to inflammation, scarring; abnormal tubal motility is less common.
Risk Factors Includes previous ectopic pregnancy, PID, endometriosis, pelvic/tubal surgery, IUD use, progesterone effects.
Pathology Uterus may be enlarged and soft; tubal ectopic most common in ampullary portion.
